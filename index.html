Bridging the Divide: The Eveleigh Bridge Project

For over a century, the railway corridor has served as the industrial heart of Sydney. Today, it stands as a physical barrier keeping neighbours apart. We are campaigning to finally build the bridge that will reunite across Eveleigh.

Support the Bridge View the Masterplan

The Great Divide: Why We Need the Bridge

The rail corridor between Redfern and Erskineville creates a 3.5-kilometre physical barrier. It is a 46-minute walk to go around it — cutting off neighbours and making simple trips a major chore.

Reconnecting Our Suburbs

  • North Side: Darlington, Newtown, Chippendale, Ultimo, Sydney University, and Carriageworks.
  • South Side: Alexandria, Erskineville, Waterloo, South Eveleigh (CBA/Mirvac), and the local high school.

The "11-Minute Save"

Moving between Carriageworks and the Large Erecting Shop:

  • Today: 1.2km trek taking 16 minutes.
  • With the Bridge: Under 5 minutes.

Growing Pains

  • Active travel must replace car detours.
  • Residents could walk to Eveleigh Markets or Carriageworks easily.
  • Cyclists could move seamlessly toward Sydney University.

Our Mission: Build the Promised Link

Renewal plans reserve space for a bridge but provide no plan to build it. We are fighting to turn that reserved space into a reality — delivering a safe, modern pedestrian and cycle link.

The Proposed Bridge

The proposal aligns with the 1942 pedestrian bridge location and sits within land already reserved in renewal plans.
