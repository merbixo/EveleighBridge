Bridging the Divide: The Eveleigh Bridge Project

For over a century, the railway corridor has served as the industrial heart of Sydney. Today, it stands as a physical barrier keeping neighbours apart. We are campaigning to finally build the bridge that will reunite across Eveleigh.

Support the Bridge View the Masterplan

The Great Divide: Why We Need the Bridge

The rail corridor between Redfern and Erskineville creates a 3.5-kilometre physical barrier. It's a 46-minute walk around the barrier, cutting off neighbours and making simple trips a major chore.

Reconnecting Our Suburbs

  • North Side: Darlington, Newtown, Chippendale, Ultimo, Sydney University, and Carriageworks.
  • South Side: Alexandria, Erskineville, Waterloo, South Eveleigh (CBA/Mirvac), and the local high school.

Our Mission: Build the Promised Link

The government’s renewal plans reserve space for a bridge but provide no plan to build it. The Eveleigh Bridge Alliance is fighting to turn that "reserved space" into a reality. We are advocating for a safe, modern pedestrian and cycle link that honors the 2004 promise.

The Proposed Bridge: Connecting the Precincts

The Eveleigh Bridge Alliance advocates for a direct pedestrian and cycle link at the heart of Eveleigh. This proposal sits almost exactly where a pedestrian bridge stood in 1942.

Technical Snapshot

  • Height: ~9 metres above the rail lines
  • Access: Ramps and stairs for 100% accessibility
  • Integration: Designed to blend with heritage architecture

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do we need a bridge now?
The area has shifted from a closed industrial site to a dense residential and commercial suburb. The current "detour" is no longer sustainable for a walkable city.
Wasn’t this bridge already planned?
Yes. In 2004, the NSW Premier promised a bridge. While the land around it is being redeveloped, the vital link was never built.
Will the bridge impact heritage buildings?
The Alliance advocates for a design that respects the NSW Heritage Register listing. A bridge enhances heritage by allowing better access.
