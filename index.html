Bridging the Divide: Eveleigh Bridge Project

For over a century, the railway corridor has served as the industrial heart of Sydney. Today, it is a physical barrier separating neighbouring communities. The Eveleigh Bridge Alliance supports building a bridge to reconnect the precincts safely and efficiently.

Join the Alliance View the Masterplan

The Great Divide: Why a Bridge is Needed

The rail corridor between Redfern and Erskineville creates a 3.5-kilometre barrier, requiring a 46-minute detour on foot. This limits pedestrian access between key local precincts.

Communities Affected

  • North Side: Darlington, Newtown, Chippendale, Ultimo, Sydney University, Carriageworks.
  • South Side: Alexandria, Erskineville, Waterloo, South Eveleigh (CBA/Mirvac), local high school.

Our Mission

The government’s redevelopment plans reserve space for a pedestrian bridge, but no construction plan exists. The Alliance supports developing a safe, accessible bridge for pedestrians and cyclists in line with prior planning commitments.

Proposed Bridge

The proposal supports a direct pedestrian and cycle connection, almost at the same location as the 1942 pedestrian bridge, while respecting heritage structures.

Technical Snapshot

  • Height: ~9 metres above rail lines
  • Access: Ramps and stairs for full accessibility
  • Integration: Designed to complement heritage architecture
