Bridging the Divide: The Eveleigh Bridge Project

For over a century, the railway corridor has served as the industrial heart of Sydney. Today, it stands as a physical barrier keeping neighbours apart. We aim to build a bridge that reconnects the community across Eveleigh.

Support the Bridge View the Masterplan

The Great Divide: Why We Need the Bridge

The rail corridor between Redfern and Erskineville creates a 3.5-kilometre physical barrier. It requires a 46-minute walk around the barrier, limiting access between local communities.

Reconnecting Our Suburbs

  • North Side: Darlington, Newtown, Chippendale, Ultimo, Sydney University, and Carriageworks.
  • South Side: Alexandria, Erskineville, Waterloo, South Eveleigh (CBA/Mirvac), and the local high school.

Our Mission: Build the Promised Link

The government’s redevelopment plans reserve space for a pedestrian bridge but provide no construction plan. The Eveleigh Bridge Alliance supports developing a safe, accessible bridge consistent with the 2004 planning commitments.

The Proposed Bridge: Connecting the Precincts

The bridge would provide a direct pedestrian and cycle link at Eveleigh, nearly aligned with a bridge that existed in 1942.

Technical Snapshot

  • Height: ~9 metres above the rail lines
  • Access: Ramps and stairs for 100% accessibility
  • Integration: Designed to blend with heritage architecture

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do we need a bridge now?
The area has transitioned from a closed industrial site to a dense residential and commercial precinct. Walking around the barrier is no longer practical.
Wasn’t this bridge already planned?
In 2004, the NSW Premier allocated space for a pedestrian bridge, but construction was never carried out.
Will the bridge impact heritage buildings?
The proposed bridge design respects heritage-listed buildings while improving access and connectivity.
