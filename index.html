Bridging the Divide: The Eveleigh Bridge Project

For over a century, the railway corridor has separated communities. This project proposes a pedestrian and cycle bridge to reconnect Darlington, Newtown, Erskineville, and surrounding precincts.

Support the Bridge View the Masterplan

The Great Divide: Why We Need the Bridge

The railway corridor between Redfern and Erskineville forms a 3.5 km barrier. Pedestrians currently take a 46-minute detour, limiting walkability and connectivity.

Reconnecting Our Suburbs

  • North Side: Darlington, Newtown, Chippendale, Ultimo, Sydney University, Carriageworks.
  • South Side: Alexandria, Erskineville, Waterloo, South Eveleigh, local high school.

Our Mission: Build the Promised Link

The government’s redevelopment plans include reserved space for a bridge, but no construction plan has been implemented. The Eveleigh Bridge Alliance supports a safe, accessible pedestrian and cycle link aligned with prior planning commitments.

The Proposed Bridge: Connecting the Precincts

The proposal creates a direct pedestrian and cycle link, historically consistent with a 1942 bridge location, fully integrating with the heritage environment.

Technical Snapshot

  • Height: ~9 metres above rail lines
  • Access: Ramps and stairs for full accessibility
  • Integration: Blends with heritage architecture

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do we need a bridge now?
The area has transitioned to a dense residential and commercial precinct. The current detour is no longer practical for walking or cycling.
Wasn’t this bridge already planned?
Yes. In 2004, the NSW Premier allocated space for a bridge. Redevelopment has occurred, but the link remains unbuilt.
Will the bridge affect heritage buildings?
The proposed design respects NSW Heritage Register listings while improving access and connectivity.
