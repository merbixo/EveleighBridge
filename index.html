Bridging the Divide: The Eveleigh Bridge Project

For more than a century, the Eveleigh rail corridor has shaped Sydney’s industrial history. Today, while both North and South Eveleigh have been revitalised, the rail lines remain a physical barrier. The Alliance is advocating for a practical pedestrian and cycle connection to restore this link.

Support the Bridge View the Masterplan

The Great Divide: Why We Need the Bridge

The rail corridor creates a 3.5-kilometre barrier between Redfern and Erskineville. It is a 46-minute walk around the rail yards.

The “11-Minute Save”

  • Today: 16-minute walk between Carriageworks and South Eveleigh.
  • With Bridge: Under 5 minutes.

Reconnecting Our Suburbs

  • North: Darlington, Newtown, Sydney University, Carriageworks.
  • South: Alexandria, Waterloo, South Eveleigh.

Eveleigh at a Glance

North Eveleigh: Arts, Culture & Housing — 400,000+ visitors annually.

South Eveleigh: Innovation & Tech Hub — 20,000+ daily workers.

Despite revitalisation on both sides, the heavy rail corridor continues to restrict direct pedestrian movement.

Our Mission: Build the Promised Link

Planning frameworks have already reserved space for a pedestrian bridge. The Alliance seeks funding allocation and formal inclusion within Transport for NSW scope.

The Proposed Bridge

The proposal aligns with the 1942 pedestrian bridge location and current reserved planning space.

  • ~9m above rail lines
  • Ramps & stairs for full accessibility
  • Potential Wilson Street cycleway connection

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do we need a bridge now?
With growing residential and employment density, current detours are no longer sustainable.
Wasn’t this bridge already planned?
Yes. Promised in 2004. The land is reserved but construction has not been funded.
Is space available?
Yes. Renewal plans have identified and reserved a location.
