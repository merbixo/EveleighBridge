Frequently Asked Questions

Why is a bridge needed now?

Residential density and employment numbers have increased significantly. Internal connectivity has not been restored since rail consolidation.

Was a bridge previously proposed?

Yes. Planning references date back to 2004. Current documents continue to reserve space for a crossing.

Is there space allocated?

Yes. Renewal plans identify landing locations on both sides of the corridor.

Will heritage be affected?

Any proposal would require compliance with NSW Heritage Register requirements and sensitive design integration.

Is construction feasible?

Pedestrian bridges over active rail lines are standard infrastructure projects when appropriate safety and engineering controls are applied.

Who comprises the Alliance?

A coalition of local community organisations advocating for delivery of the reserved link.
